Miami, FL

Uhh... its hot

I grew up and have spent almost all of my life in the small town of Miami, Florida. I got an immense amount of culture shock when I arrived to Worcester again for this program because of the sheer lack of Latinos here, contrasting significantly with Miami's almost 70% Hispanic population.

In my view, Miami is sunny, festive, and unique; you can't expect anything that is commonplace elsewhere to exist at all there. The same goes for things that are commonplace in Miami. I've heard the city referred to as a bubble, and for good reason. I was startled by the fact that no one knew what a "Publix" was up here.

Although we don't have stereotypical "Florida Men" down here, we have a great sense of culture and even better food, creating a great community as a whole.
