Daily Progress

Day 1 - 11/7 - Initial Prototyping

I worked on creating a cardboard prototype of the small clinostat to find the scale needed to fit our smaller panels inside. We decided to scrap this cardboard design later after finding a scalable model online in day 3. Prior to this point, our team was working on testing out power systems for the LED and getting motors to work on the arduino.

Day 2 - 11/12 - Initial Design Concept

The group mostly progressed on the Initial Design Concept document. The second half of class, we ran into the problem of the motor wires tangling without a designated stop location in the code. I researched how other rotating applications solved this problem, and informed my group about using a slip ring.

Day 3 - 11/14 - Initial Prototype Production & Assembly

After finding out about the slip ring, we found a scalable model clinostat online to fit our panels. We decided to not resize it for the first print to have a working prototype for our project mentor meeting next class. I was able to prepare, start, and finish printing in the same day, and we had a working prototype with small slip rings over the weekend.

Day 4 - 11/18 - Initial Prototype Showcase

We demonstrated our initial prototype clinostat to one of our mentors, Mr. Mario Cruz, in a zoom meeting during class. There, we demonstrated our ideas going further and what we would do during and after Thanksgiving Break. After the meeting, I began sketching out the dimensions of the second prototype in OnShape.

Day 5 - 11/20 -

We converted the 180 degree servos on the initial prototype to continuous rotation servos for further testing before we set up the larger, light panel compatible prototype. After some testing and code adjustment, I began to rescale the initial prototype to fit the second prototype's panel testing requirement.

Day 6 - 11/22 - Website and Logo Work

I worked on keeping my website and documents up to date for housekeeping. Later on in the day, I finished my 2D CAD designed logo, uploaded it to my website, and made a vinyl sticker out of it with the school's Cricut.
